What about me?

Martin Garrix is a Dutch electronic music producer whose multi-platinum dance anthems have topped charts around the globe. He was introduced to electronic dance music at the age of 8 when he saw Tiësto perform at the Olympic Games opening ceremony on live TV.

Career

Martin Garrix's career as an Electronic Dance Music (EDM) star began at the age of 14 when he served as a disc jockey at his high school dance. After meeting Tiësto and having him as a mentor, he released his first tracks, 'BFAM' (with Julian Jordan) and 'Just Some Loops' (with TV Noise).

In 2012, he released 'Error 404' (with Jay Hardway), his first track through 'Spinnin' Records'. The following year, he released 'Torrent' (with Sidney Samson) on Tiësto's own label, Musical Freedom. His first solo work, 'Animals', was released as a digital download on June 16, 2013, and became a mega hit, topping multiple charts in Europe. It also made Garrix the youngest person to have a song reach the No.1 spot on the electronic music store 'Beatport'.

Martin Garrix has achieved numerous milestones in his career. He performed alongside Ed Sheeran at the Ultra Music Festival in Miami in 2015. In mid-2015, he left 'Spinnin' Records' due to a dispute over the ownership of his music. In early 2016, he launched his own label, 'STMPD RCRDS', and released his new track 'Now That I've Found You' through the label. His promotional single 'Opps', released on June 13, 2016, was chosen as the anthem for the 'Electronic Entertainment Expo 2016'.
